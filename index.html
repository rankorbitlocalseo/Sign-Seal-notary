Understanding the Role of a Notary Public

A Notary Public is a state-appointed official responsible for serving as an impartial witness during the signing of important documents. Their role is to verify the identity of the signer, ensure that all parties are willing participants, and confirm that the document is signed willingly and knowingly. Whether it's a real estate agreement, a legal affidavit, or an adoption form, notarization adds a layer of trust and legal credibility.

What Is Remote Online Notarization (RON)?

Remote online notarization, or RON, is the process by which a Notary Public notarizes documents using secure digital platforms. It involves video conferencing, identity verification tools, electronic signatures, and digital stamps. The entire process is designed to mimic the traditional notarization process while allowing the signer and notary to be in different locations.

It's important to note that not all states support RON, and even within Idaho, the notary must be authorized by the Secretary of State to perform such services. A reputable Notary Public in Nampa ID who offers remote notarizations will typically inform clients of their credentials and the legal guidelines involved.

Legal Requirements for Remote Notarization in Idaho

In Idaho, remote notarization is permitted under the Uniform Law on Notarial Acts. The law mandates the use of secure audio-visual technology and compliant software platforms. The notary must also keep an audio-visual recording of the session and a digital log of each notarial act.

When you reach out to a Notary Public in Nampa ID for remote notarization, ensure they meet these state requirements. This will help protect you from any legal challenges that might arise from improperly notarized documents.
